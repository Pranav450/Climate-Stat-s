Climate Change

It is a change which is attributed directly or indirectly by human activity that alters the composition of the global atmosphere and which is in addition to the natural climate variability observed over comparative time periods. .

Reasons

Greenhouse gases are present in the atmosphere and absorb heat radiation, which they radiate back towards the earth's surface. The most important man-made greenhouse gas is carbon dioxide (CO2). It is caused by the burning of fossil fuels and changes in land use, such as deforestation. .
